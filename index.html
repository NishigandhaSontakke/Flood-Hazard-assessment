Flood Ready

Flood Hazard analysis for Building infrastructure
City of Ashland WI

By Nishigandha Sontakke

Why?

A recently realsed study by Northwest Regional Planning Commission has found that floods in northern wisconsin can cause up to $24 million in damage to more than 1,300 homes and businesses. Planning, preparing, and responding to such flood disasters requires an understanding of the unique risks and vulnerabilities within each community. This is especially true due to recent heavy rainfall events that have produced floods that have exceeded historical levels. To effectively assess and plan for such hazards, better building data is needed that goes beyond the current generalized data used in some flood models. Spatially accurate data on building footprints in combination with flood hazard information, will allow to better model the risks and vulnerabilities to the building infrastructure in a given community.

Study Area

Ashland is a city in Ashland county in the U.S. state of Wisconsin. The city is a port on Lake Superior, near the head of Chequamegon Bay. The population was 8,216 at the 2010 census, all of which resided in the Ashland County portion of the city.

Storm Sewer Capacity Assessment

As study conducted by City of Ashland, Project No. 6270701 "Trunk storm sewer pipes had approximately 10-yr capacity while trunk storm sewer inlets had approximately 5-yr capacity. However, because high capacity storm sewers were frequently matched with low capacity inlets, and vice-versa, the overall average capacity of the storm sewer system was found to be capable of collecting and conveying peak flows from the 2-yr rainfall event. Note that the reported average values are, in actuality, the median values, so there are many locations in the City where the capacity is substantially less than the averages reported here."
Further according to report 10-yr service, most frequently the minimum design standard for municipal storm sewer systems.Estimated cost to install additional and/or higher capacity storm drainage infrastructure to provide 2-yr capacity was $3.5M, the cost to provide 5-yr capacity is $4.6M, and the cost to provide 10-yr capacity is $6.1M.
